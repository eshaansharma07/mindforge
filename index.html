Rules & Guidelines

Official rulebook for MindForge 2026. Read carefully before participating in any round.

Event Overview

MindForge is a multi-stage competitive technical event designed to test speed, logical thinking, coding skills, and debugging ability.

  • Round 1: 60-Second Showdown
  • Round 2: Sudden Coding
  • Round 3: Debug Relay Race

Team Structure

  • Each team can have 1 to 4 participants.
  • Only one laptop or device per team is allowed.
  • Device sharing is allowed, but only one person operates at a time.
  • No switching or adding devices during the event.
  • Teams must register with final members. No changes later.
  • Teams must stay together throughout all rounds.

General Rules

  • Participants must carry college ID, laptop, and charger.
  • Internet usage is not allowed unless explicitly permitted.
  • Only one submission per team per round is allowed.
  • Late submissions will not be accepted.
  • Judges' decisions are final and binding.

Strictly Prohibited

  • Plagiarism
  • Communication with other teams
  • Use of AI tools, internet, or external help unless allowed
  • Misconduct or unfair means

Round 1 Format

  • Platform: Custom Website
  • Questions: 10
  • Time Limit: 150 seconds
  • Questions may be MCQ, logic-based, or output-based.

Round 1 Rules

  • Timer starts immediately after quiz begins.
  • No pause or restart is allowed.
  • Auto-submit after time ends.
  • Only one attempt per team.
  • Evaluation is based on correct answers and time as tie-breaker.

Round 2 Format

  • Mode: Offline Coding
  • Platform: Personal laptops
  • Questions and test cases are shared through the event portal.
  • Teams solve locally and submit final testcase outputs.

Round 2 Rules

  • Internet usage is not allowed.
  • Any offline IDE or compiler is allowed.
  • Teams must write code locally and execute test cases.
  • Only final outputs are submitted.
  • Only one final submission is allowed.

Round 2 Evaluation

  • Based on correctness of outputs.
  • Accuracy across all test cases matters.
  • Tie-breaker: earliest correct submission.
  • No external help, AI tools, or internet usage.

Round 3 Format

  • Mode: Pen and Paper
  • Teams receive a problem statement and bug-filled code snippet.
  • No electronic devices are allowed.
  • Only provided sheets can be used.

Round 3 Evaluation

  • Identify errors accurately.
  • Correct the code logic.
  • Provide the final corrected output or explanation.
  • One final answer sheet per team.

Disqualification

  • Use of more than one device
  • Multiple submissions
  • Internet misuse or external assistance
  • Communication with other teams
  • Misconduct or any rule violation

Time & Discipline

  • Report 10 minutes before each round.
  • No extra time will be given.
  • Follow instructions from organizers and volunteers.
  • Maintain discipline and professionalism.

Judging & Results

  • Advancement depends on round performance.
  • Final winners are decided by overall performance.
  • Tie-break priority: Round 2 score, then Round 1, then Round 3.
  • Any argument or disruption may lead to disqualification.

Organizer Rights

  • Organizers may modify rules if necessary.
  • Organizers may disqualify teams violating rules.
  • Organizers reserve the right to resolve disputes.
  • Think fast. Code smart. Debug smarter.
